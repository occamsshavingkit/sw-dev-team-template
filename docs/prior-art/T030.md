## 5. Known pitfalls

- Role shadowing: avoid `coder`/`reviewer`/provider-native roles that bypass `software-engineer`, `code-reviewer`, or `tech-lead` ownership.
- Customer-interface drift: OpenCode must not create a second customer-facing path; only top-level `tech-lead` talks to the customer.
- Authority drift: adapter docs must cite canonical inputs instead of becoming independent policy.
- Harness-specific overfit: preserve harness-neutral role semantics and keep OpenCode-specific behavior in the adapter ADR or generated adapter layer.

## 6. Recommendation to `architect` / `software-engineer`

Write the ADR as an adapter decision: OpenCode may configure provider/model routing and thin wrappers, but it must read and preserve the canonical role contracts from `CLAUDE.md`, `AGENTS.md`, and `.claude/agents/*.md`; reject any option that introduces a parallel role roster or customer interface.
