## 8. Duel

**Solution Duel** — pre-code adversarial review. `qa-engineer` writes
three concrete ways this proposal could fail before implementation.

### 8.1 Findings (`qa-engineer`)

- **F-1 [Major]:** The ADR can still fail in production if it records OpenCode as an adapter but does not define a concrete negative check for parallel role rosters; a later implementer could add OpenCode-native `coder` / `reviewer` roles that bypass `software-engineer`, `code-reviewer`, or `tech-lead` while claiming the ADR was satisfied.
- **F-2 [Major]:** The ADR can preserve the customer-interface rule in prose yet omit where OpenCode-specific dispatch, fallback, and wrapper guidance belongs; that leaves `AGENTS.md`, runtime adapter docs, and generated wrappers free to drift into competing source hierarchies after the ADR lands.
- **F-3 [Minor]:** The proposal defers the chosen ADR alternative to `architect` without naming acceptance criteria for the chosen option, so implementation can start after an ADR exists but before QA can verify it rejected role shadowing, customer-interface drift, and harness-specific overfit from `docs/prior-art/T030.md`.

### 8.2 Rebuttals / revisions (`software-engineer`)

- **F-1 closed:** The ADR verification section now gives the negative check: no OpenCode-native `coder`, `reviewer`, or similar role may bypass `software-engineer`, `code-reviewer`, or `tech-lead` authority.
- **F-2 closed:** The ADR places OpenCode-specific routing in `docs/model-routing-guidelines.md` and thin-wrapper guidance in `AGENTS.md`; both remain subordinate to canonical role files.
- **F-3 closed:** ADR acceptance criteria now cover role-shadowing rejection, customer-interface preservation, and harness-specific overfit prevention.
