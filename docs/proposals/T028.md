## 8. Duel

**Solution Duel** — pre-code adversarial review. `qa-engineer` writes
three concrete ways this proposal could fail before implementation.

### 8.1 Findings (`qa-engineer`)

- **Critical:** If `docs/workflow-pipeline.md` is created but shipped role
  and template references still cite `docs/proposals/workflow-redesign-v0.12.md`,
  downstream users inherit broken links to excluded material and cannot
  reconstruct the binding workflow.
- **Major:** If historical rationale is copied into the new canonical doc
  alongside trigger and stage rules, non-binding proposal trade-offs can be
  laundered into mandatory policy and change agent behavior unexpectedly.
- **Major:** If role files keep full local copies of the pipeline rules
  instead of concise summaries plus links, future edits will drift across
  roles and agents will apply different trigger or retention rules.

### 8.2 Rebuttals / revisions (`software-engineer`)

- Addressed: `docs/workflow-pipeline.md` is now the shipped
  authority and `.claude/agents/tech-lead.md`, `.claude/agents/researcher.md`,
  `.claude/agents/security-engineer.md`, `.claude/agents/software-engineer.md`,
  `.claude/agents/architect.md`, `.claude/agents/qa-engineer.md`, and
  shipped templates point to it.
- Addressed in new canonical doc: only trigger, stage, ownership,
  retention, and escape-hatch rules moved; proposal rationale remains
  outside binding policy.
- Addressed in scoped role files: remaining binding workflow-pipeline
  references now point to `docs/workflow-pipeline.md`; historical proposal
  material remains non-binding.
