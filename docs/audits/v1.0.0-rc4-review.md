## Findings

### Fixed During Review — Manifest helper exclusion parity

Severity: Warning

`scripts/lib/manifest.sh` said its ship-file enumeration matched
`scripts/upgrade.sh`, but it did not exclude the full template-only set
used by scaffold / upgrade: `ROADMAP.md`, `docs/audits/`, `docs/v2/`,
`docs/proposals/`, `docs/v1.0-rc3-checklist.md`, and
`docs/pm/process-audit-*.md`.

Impact: mostly latent because scaffolded projects omit those paths
before manifest hashes are written, but the helper contract was weaker
than its comment and could confuse future manifest verification work.

Resolution: `manifest_ship_files` now mirrors the relevant
template-only exclusions from `upgrade.sh` while retaining the new
`.claude/agents/*-local.md` exclusion.

### #59 Aggregate Backlog — No New rc4 Blocker

Severity: Observation

Issue #59 is a broad release-candidate backlog, not a single defect.
The scan found that several subitems already landed in earlier releases
or adjacent ADRs/templates:

- Bare requirements / architecture variants and per-item / per-view
  breakouts are represented by FW-ADR-0003 / FW-ADR-0004 and shipped
  templates.
- External-reference adoption, URL-only inventory rationale, and the
  "inspire, don't paste" posture are represented by FW-ADR-0007 and
  `docs/glossary/ENGINEERING.md`.
- Token tracking exists through `docs/templates/task-template.md` DoD
  and `docs/templates/pm/TOKEN_LEDGER-template.md`.

Remaining #59 items are still real backlog, but they are broad additive
standards/template work: token-budget fields, full IEEE 1044 / 730 /
1012 paraphrase adoption, AI/ML requirements expansion, deeper
requirements/architecture reshaping, and agent-definition
consolidation. None is needed to close the #71-#83 rc4 stabilization
set unless the customer explicitly promotes it.

Recommendation: keep #59 open as post-rc4 / v1.0.x-v1.1 backlog, or
split the remaining subitems into focused issues after rc4 tags.
